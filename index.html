What is Blockchain Currency?

Blockchain currency (like ETH) is digital money that exists on the internet. It is not controlled by a bank or government. It can be used to send money directly to others safely and transparently. In this platform, it is used to fund campaigns and help people.

🌍 How people buy it in real life

  • People use real money (like ₹ or $)
  • They go to trusted apps/websites (called exchanges)
  • They pay using card, bank, or UPI
  • They receive crypto in their wallet

🎮 How it works here (Demo version)

  • Users go to the "Buy Crypto" section in their Dashboard
  • Enter an amount in their local currency (₹, $, etc.)
  • See how much ETH they would receive (approximate value)
  • Click "Buy Now" to simulate a purchase

⚠️ This is a demo feature. No real money or cryptocurrency is used.

What is Blockchain & How Does This Work?

You might have heard of "Blockchain" or "Smart Contracts". Here is a simple explanation of what they are and how they keep your money 100% safe on this platform.

🔗

The Blockchain

Think of a blockchain as a public, unhackable record book that everyone in the world can see. Every time someone donates, or money is sent to a beneficiary, it is written in this book permanently. Nobody can erase or fake a transaction.

🤖

Smart Contracts

A smart contract is like a robot accountant. It holds the donated money safely in a digital vault. It has strict rules written in code: "Do not release the money unless the majority of donors vote Yes." It follows the rules exactly—no human can force it to give up the money early.

🛡️

Why It Matters

On traditional websites, a company holds your money and can run away with it or block the transfer. Here, there is no company. The robot accountant (Smart Contract) pays the needy directly, ensuring your money always goes exactly where you intended.
